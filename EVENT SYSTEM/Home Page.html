WHAT IS THIS?
An event website for a College of Engineering is an essential resource designed to connect students, faculty, alumni, and industry professionals with a wide range of activities and opportunities within the engineering community. This platform typically features an organized event calendar that lists upcoming workshops, guest lectures, career fairs, and conferences, making it easy for users to find relevant information.

Moments of CET
A day where students present groundbreaking projects, culminating in an award for the most innovative idea. A team unveils a prototype of a sustainable energy device that captures audience attention, leading to a partnership with a local startup. Engineering students team up with peers from other disciplines (like arts and sciences) to tackle complex problems, resulting in unexpected and innovative solutions, such as a wearable health monitor designed by engineering and design students together.
One simple yet impactful event that could be held at the College of Engineering is a Lunch and Learn session, where students and faculty come together to share a meal while exploring a specific engineering topic. This event fosters an informal environment conducive to learning and discussion, allowing participants to delve into recent industry trends, innovative research, or even exciting student projects. By inviting guest speakers, such as industry professionals or alumni, the sessions can provide valuable insights into real-world applications of engineering principles, bridging the gap between academic theory and practical experience.

ABOUT
The College of Engineering and Technology was one of the departments initially established in 1995 There were various courses offered such as; BS in Civil Engineering, BS in Electrical Engineering, BS in Electronics Engineering, BS in Industrial Engineering, BS in Computer Engineering, and BS in Computer Science. Presently, the CET offers BS in Electronics Engineering and BS in Computer Engineering, the only programs retained since the college’s establishment. The BS in Information Technology also presently offered by the University replaced BS in Computer Science Years later, other fields of specialization such as Data Science and Cybersecurity were added to BSIT.
The College of Engineering and Technology (CET) is committed to the growth of the Manileño’s by developing Information Technology and Engineering professionals, entrepreneurs, managers, and new technologies. We offer programs that are sought after hence our graduates are employable. The College of Engineering and Technology’s strong alliance to industry ensures relevancy in classrooms and research that support our mission of building solutions for a strong economy and a sustainable environment. The College of Engineering and Technology is represented by the color Orange stimulating the intensity in systems development, rising infrastructures, and digitization that its students and faculty are known for. As a mood-lifting color, it also creates an image of optimistic innovation.
